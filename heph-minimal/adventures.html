Hades

Copyright © 2001 by Mark Hughes <kamikaze@kuoi.asui.uidaho.edu>

GAME OVER

You have died, but your adventure continues, in the cold, quiet, grey world of Hades. There is only one chance to return to your life - descend through the levels of Hades, fighting your way past the guardians, until you discover the one item which will permit your escape.

Hades is an adaptation of the classic computer game Rogue for Hephaestus.

DESTROY ALL POCKET MONSTERS!

Copyright © 2002 by Mark Hughes <kamikaze@kuoi.asui.uidaho.edu>

Designed as a tutorial, Pocket is still a hard little combat-focused adventure, with a social message that affects each and every one of us. You play Mark, an angry and vengeful programmer, out to destroy an infestation of pocket monsters, led by the gigantic jaundiced rodent King Kichapu! The social message, of course, is that pocket monsters are really annoying.

FiveRings

Copyright © 2002 by Mark Hughes <kamikaze@kuoi.asui.uidaho.edu>

COMING SOON!

A thousand years ago, a god descended to your world from the void. It was called Apocalypse. Its passage caused volcanoes to erupt, dug chasms in the Earth from which poured nightmares made flesh, and civilization was all but annihilated...

In the final hours, the Ancients completed a great weapon and trapped Apocalypse in an endless slumber, neither alive nor dead.

To prevent the weapon from being misused, but knowing that it might be needed again one day, they created five rings of power, one for each of the elements, which would unlock the fortress at the end of the world, and hid those rings in well-protected dungeons throughout the world.

The nightmare god now sleeps fitfully, and its dreams plague the world. From every nation, heroes are sent out to seek the rings of power and prevent Apocalypse from waking.

An early beta version of Five Rings is available now, but will probably not be finished for several more weeks.

Olympus

Copyright © 2001-2002 by Mark Hughes <kamikaze@kuoi.asui.uidaho.edu>

COMING IN 2002!

The world is a barren wasteland of red sand, sustained only by canals from the polar ice caps. The Throwbacks abandon civilization and become nomads and brigands, ripping up train tracks and telegraph wires, pillaging and burning towns as they prepare for the end of the world.

Civilized people huddle in increasingly fortress-like cities, and prepare in their own way - with increasing decadence and introspection, as the machinery of society breaks down.

Why do the gods no longer answer your prayers?

Why is the world dying?
